La Casa de Papel, a Spanish series distributed throughout the world by Netflix, has been drawing public attention by telling the story of a group of strangers who come together to carry out the greatest robbery in history. With captivating characters both among the group of criminals, as well as between the hostages and the police, it can be confusing to decorate who is who. Interpreted by Pedro Alonso, Berlin is the commander in charge of the operation at the Mint. He is a thief who specializes in stealing jewels and entered the world of crime to maintain the high standard of living to which he had been accustomed since childhood. Berlin believes it is superior to the rest of the gang and despises the others internally. He also knows how to be violent when he needs to.

Interpreted by Álvaro Morte, the character is the brain of the operation and a man who passes an image that nobody would ever suspect is involved in some criminal plot. In addition, he is very different from any of the individuals who make up his gang. With above average intelligence, he has planned every detail of the operation for years.

Performed by Úrsula Corberó is a strong, impulsive woman who does everything to pretend not to call anyone. Tokyo entered the world of crime at the age of 14 in the footsteps of her boyfriend and since then has alternated periods of normalcy with conventional jobs with others linked to all kinds of assaults. One of them ended up in a shooting with three deaths, including that of her boyfriend.

Itziar Ituño interprets Raquel, the police inspector responsible for the negotiation of the kidnapping. This case comes at a worse time, as she is experiencing severe problems with her ex-husband over domestic violence and custody of her daughter, Paula, just 7 years old. While going through this bad phase, she feels bound to see the criminals predicting every step she takes.

Miguel Herrán plays Rio, a hacker boy who knows everything about programming and piracy. This ability and this facility to learn such things made him become chief technician of a security company when he was still very young. However, all this knowledge was not accompanied by a compatible degree of maturity - it was already sought by Interpol.

Roberto García plays Oslo, a Serbian who represents the brute strength of the group next to Helsinki. Like Oslo, little is known about it and it is also not much to ask questions before the difficulties that appear in the present. Helsinki and he are great soldiers for the Teacher's plan.

Paco Tous plays Moscow, a man who worked in mines and was removed from the profession due to asthma and ended up in a locksmith shop. He is able to do anything with metal and with that, open any padlock. This skill and poverty led him into the world of crime - he was convicted of the theft of several jewelry stores.

Darko Peric plays Helsinki, a Serbian who represents the brute strength of the group next to Oslo. Little is known about him, apart from having a past punctuated by violence. He has little inclination to dialogue, and he and Oslo are the perfect combat soldiers for the Teacher's plan.

Played by Jaime Lorente, Denver is the son of Moscow. He's a dangerously impulsive boy. You've used every possible drug, and maybe it has altered your way of looking at things and makes them explosive. His father is his only anchor with the human side and his feelings, since he would do anything for Moscow.

Alba Flores plays Nairobi, a young woman who had a difficult childhood and decided to make a living by making fakes. When she was pregnant, still in her teens, she was abandoned by a boyfriend and saw no option but the crime. After going to prison, she lost custody of the child. With the coup in the Mint, she plans to retrieve her son and go to a quiet place.

Fran Morcillo plays Pablo, a 17-year-old teenager. He is a handsome boy by whom all the girls are in love. Full of self-confidence, he likes to be the center of attention. He looks like a nice guy about to start a relationship with Alison the day they go on a field trip to the Mint, but things go dark.

Fernando Soto plays Ángel, the inspector. Despite nearly 30 years in action, he has never felt as intimidated as ever since he first met Rachel. At the time, the two were dealing with a man on a bridge threatening to kill himself. Rachel's ability to convince him to come down in record time impressed Angel in every possible way.

Maria Pedraza plays Alison Parker, daughter of the British ambassador, a shy teenager, nerd and suffocated by the overprotection of all. When Pablo, the most handsome boy in his class, declares himself to her on the bus on the way to an Mint tour, Alison does not think twice - but the following events will make her regret.

Juan Fernández plays Colonel Prieto, whose main objective is to release hostage Alison Parker as quickly as possible to prevent the kidnapping from becoming an international incident, since she is the daughter of an English diplomat. But the task will not be easy, since his relationship with Raquel, the one in charge of negotiation, was never the easiest.

Arturo Román, the director of Casa da Moeda, is played by Enrique Arce. He considers himself a good boss and a good husband, but he is really just a stingy, frightened man, willing to do whatever it takes to survive. It's like a mouse ready to leave the ship and whoever is inside it.

Esther Acebo plays Monica Gaztambide, a woman who became a very costly secretary to the Director of the Mint. The problem is that she gets involved with Arturo, her married and children, and discovers that she is pregnant with him shortly before the workplace is invaded by the kidnappers.
